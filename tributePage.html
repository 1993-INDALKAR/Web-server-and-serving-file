Dadasaheb Phalke

The Father of Indian cinema

Dadasaheb Phalke with his cameraman

Here's a time line of Dadasaheb's life:

  • 1870 - Born in Nashik, India
  • 1885 - Joined Sir J. J. School of Art, Mumbai
  • 1890 - After passing from J.J. School in 1890, Phalke went to the Kala Bhavan, Maharaja Sayajirao University of Baroda in Vadodara, where he studied sculpture, engineering, drawing, painting and photography
  • 1912 - Made his first film, Raja Harishchandra
  • 1913 - Movie was shown publicly on 3 May 1913 at Mumbai's Coronation Cinema
  • 1932 - His last silent movie Setubandhan was released
  • 1994 - dies at the age of 73.
